BIOGRAPHY


I am devoted to constantly challenging and improving myself.

1991 - Alternative Culture

I discovered live music and the legendary concert venue First Avenue in Minneapolis when I got my license to drive at age 16 in 1992. I've been collecting vinyl albums and concert experiences ever since. Throughout high school I attended every all-ages concert I could find. I befriended a large crew of friends in the city.

1994 - Minneapolis

I worked as a line cook at two collectively-managed vegan cafes. I was introduced to graffiti and raves, fell in love with the lifestyle, and travelled throughout the midwest attending legendary massives in Milwaukee, Chicago, Detroit.

1997 - Olympia, Washington

I was a member of a local Art and Activism group. I was intimately involved with the people that were responsible for the 1999 Seattle WTO protests. I was involved with west coast raves and west coast graffiti until the fun petered out after 9/11.

2000 - Brooklyn, New York

I had the unique experience of watching the tragedy of 9/11 from my apartment building's rooftop in downtown Brooklyn. I attended the world's premier vegan health-supportive culinary school, the Natural Gourmet Institute. I took technology classes in my spare time at New School University. In New York I befriended a large group of young people born and raised in the city. I taught myself HTML in the year 2000.

2005 - Ashford, Connecticut

The years 2005 through 2008 I was the Veggie Chef at The Hole In The Wall Gang Camp in Ashford, Connecticut. The Hole in the Wall serves sick children and their families and has grown to affect tens of thousands of children around the world! I cooked lunch and dinner every day at camp. I was fortunate enough to prepare and serve meals to Paul, his wife Joann and their family and friends regularly.

2009 - Seattle, Washington

Line cook extraordinaire at the hippest and best health-food restaurants in Seattle. I worked at two award winning vegan restaurants in the city. I volunteered in my free time feeding the homeless and less fortunate. I volunteered at a local radio station.

2012 - Indianapolis, Indiana

After ten years straight of cooking professionally I wanted a change. I was offered the position Business Manager at eGov Strategies, my brother-in-law's company. Everyday was a learning experience and everyday was different. This is when I first dreamt up my Multisport Decathlon event.

2015 - River Falls, Wisconsin

I loved bartending! And I was a natural! I learned so much about myself and others, it was an invaluable experience. Bartending at a classic townie dive bar in a classic wisconsin college town, I had no idea what I had in store when I applied for this job. I'm empathetic, a great listener, genuine, warm, clean, fast, always smiling and positive, loved to give great service. It was so fun.

2020 - Indianapolis, Indiana

1150 Coding Bootcamp and finally taking my lifelong love of technology and computers to the next level. Knowing full stack development would also mean I can get my dream off the ground. I've also never been more determined to launch my Multisport Decathlon sports event.
